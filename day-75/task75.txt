Understanding how the internet works is essential for every developer, given the web's integral role in modern technology. Here’s a comprehensive breakdown based on Kamran Ahmed's guide:

### What is the Internet?
The Internet is a global network of interconnected computers that communicate via standardized protocols. It's a vast and decentralized system that no single person or organization controls.

### How Information Moves on the Internet
Information travels as bits (binary data) over various mediums:
- **Ethernet Cables:** Commonly used in wired networks, offering reliable connections.
- **Fiber Optic Cables:** Enable fast data transfer over long distances using light signals.
- **Wi-Fi (Wireless Signals):** Uses radio waves for wireless data transmission.

### IP Addresses and DNS
- **IP (Internet Protocol):** Assigns unique addresses to devices, enabling them to locate and communicate with each other.
- **DNS (Domain Name System):** Translates human-readable domain names (e.g., www.example.com) into IP addresses.

### Packets, Routing, and Reliability
Data on the Internet is split into packets that travel independently and may take different routes to reach their destination. This method ensures reliable and efficient data transfer.

### HTTP and HTML
- **HTTP (Hypertext Transfer Protocol):** The foundation of data communication on the web, enabling the transfer of webpages.
- **HTML (Hypertext Markup Language):** The standard language for creating and formatting webpages.

### Encryption and Public Keys
Cryptography ensures secure communication over the Internet. Techniques like SSL/TLS encrypt data to protect it from unauthorized access.

### Cybersecurity and Crime
Cybersecurity encompasses protective measures against criminal activities involving networks and the internet. Common threats include hacking, phishing, and malware.
